Method — Tap ▶ to start each step timer
1
Crush Spices Prep — no timer
Lightly crack elaichi pods — just open, do not powder. Roughly crush ginger. Same regardless of quantity.
2
Boil Water Alone 2 min
Add only water to vessel. HIGH flame. Bring to full rolling boil 100°C. No spices yet — pure water only.
🌡️ 100°C — full rolling boil with large bubbles
🔌 Set induction to Level 9 / MAX (1800–2200W)
02:00
Bringing water to full rolling boil...
3
Add Milk + Elaichi 5–6 min
Pour all milk into boiling water. Immediately add crushed elaichi. HIGH flame. Stir continuously. Wait for full rolling boil — milk will froth, keep stirring.
🌡️ 85°C frothing starts → 100°C rolling boil. Do not leave unattended!
🔌 Set induction to Level 8–9 (1600–1800W) — watch for overflow
05:00
Watch for frothing — stir continuously!
4
Add Ginger (Adrak) 1–2 min
As milk reaches rolling boil — add crushed ginger immediately. Stay on HIGH. Ginger at 95–100°C releases best flavour compounds.
🌡️ 95–100°C — keep full boil during this step
🔌 Keep induction at Level 8 (1600W) — maintain full boil
01:30
Ginger releasing flavour — keep HIGH!
5
Add CTC Tea 1 min — always fixed
Add Wagh Bakri CTC tea. Full rolling boil HIGH for exactly 1 minute. Deep colour, aroma and taste comes from here. Never reduce this time.
🌡️ Must be 100°C full boil — CTC needs maximum temperature
🔌 Set induction to MAX / Boost (2000–2200W) — full power
01:00
Full boil — deep colour forming!
6
Reduce & Condense 12–14 min
Lower to MEDIUM heat. Stir every 30 sec. Milk reduces to 700ml per litre — thick and creamy. Key step — patience.
1L milk → 12–14 min on medium heat (home gas)
🌡️ 82–92°C — rolling simmer, not hard boil
🔌 Set induction to Level 4–5 (800–1000W) — gentle steady simmer
14:00
Stir every 30 seconds!
🥄 Stir now!
7
Add Sugar 1 min — always fixed
Add sugar. Stir well. One final boil on HIGH. Taste and adjust sweetness.
🌡️ 90–95°C — bring back to boil after adding sugar
🔌 Set induction to Level 7 (1400W) — bring back to boil
01:00
Final boil — dissolving sugar...
8
Strain & Serve
Strain through fine mesh into 100ml glasses. Fill to 70ml mark. Serve piping hot immediately.
Yield: 10 glasses × 70ml per litre milk

What Makes It Special

Regular chai = lots of water, milk added late. Green Park Doodh Chai = almost pure milk, slow reduced to half, thick and creamy.

Full 1 minute boil of tea on HIGH flame gives the deep colour, strong aroma and bold taste. Never skip or reduce this step.

The Yield Rule

160ml milk → ~80ml chai in the glass. Always use 2× milk vs. quantity to serve. A 100ml glass served at 80ml is the authentic Indian style — room for steam, no spill.
